Date Squares

What is it?

A traditional Canadian dessert with deep roots in Canadian baking culture with British influence. It is a delicious sweet date filling sandwiched between a delectable oat crumble.

This dessert gained popularity in the early 20th century, and is still very much a staple at family gatherings.

This recipe will take 1 hour 20 minutes and yield 9-16 squares

Preheat Oven to 350 degrees F

For the Filling, you will need the following 5 ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cup Medjool Dates, pitted and chopped, packed lightly
  • 1 cup Water
  • 2 tablespoons Lemon Juice
  • 2 tablespoons Brown Sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon Baking Soda

For the Oat Crumble, you will need the following 5 ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cup Quick Cooking Oats
  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 3/4 cup Brown Sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 3/4 cup Salted Butter, softened

Steps to prepare Filling:

  1. Combine Dates, Water, Lemon Juice, and Brown Sugar in a pot and bring to a boil.
  2. Add Baking Soda and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Then remove from heat and let mixture cool.

Steps to prepare Oat Crumble:

  1. Combine Oats, Flour, Brown Sugar, and Baking Powder in a bowl.
  2. Add Butter, stir to combine.
  3. Butter and parchment line an 8 inch square baking dish.
  4. Spread half the Oat Crumble into the baking dish, press firmly.
  5. Spread on Date mixture.
  6. Cover with the remaining Oat Crumble, press lightly.
  7. Bake on oven middle rack for 55 minutes, or until golden-brown.
  8. Let cool on wired rack for 4 hours before removing from dish and cutting into squares.
  9. Best enjoyed with a cup of tea, or coffee, and good company.
